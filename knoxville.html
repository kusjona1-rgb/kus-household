Downtown & Near the Meet
Market Square · Gay Street · post-meet lunch
▼
Market Square
🚶 Walk from CC⭐ Multiple options
The heart of downtown Knoxville — a pedestrian square with 10+ restaurants, outdoor seating, and a great vibe. Perfect for the post-meet celebratory lunch. Browse and pick whatever looks good to the crew.
🗺️ Directions
The Old City / Gay Street
🚶 Near downtown⭐ 4.4+
Knoxville's revitalized historic strip with unique restaurants, coffee shops, and local character. Great for a post-meet wander. Hit Peter Kern Library (speakeasy bar) if the adults want a cocktail after.
🗺️ Directions
Tupelo Honey
🚶 Near CC⭐ 4.4 · Southern brunch
Southern comfort food elevated — biscuits, fried chicken, shrimp and grits. Knoxville staple and a great celebratory lunch option right near the Convention Center.
🗺️ Directions

Sevier Avenue
🚗 5 min drive⭐ Local favorite strip
South Knoxville's answer to downtown — a stretch of bars, restaurants, and coffee shops with a neighborhood feel. Less tourist-y than downtown. Great for Saturday evening if you want something local and laid-back. Try Blue Slip Winery, Hi-Wire Brewing, or Suttree's.

Right Out the Door
South Knoxville Urban Wilderness · walkable
▼
Baker Creek Preserve
🚶 Steps from Airbnb🐕 Dogs Welcome⭐ 4.7
A beautiful 120-acre nature preserve with gentle trails through forest and along the creek. Literally walkable from the Airbnb — perfect morning or evening stroll. Easy terrain, great for everyone in the group.
🗺️ Directions
Urban Wilderness Gateway Park
🚶 Very close🐕 Dog Friendly⭐ 4.6
50+ miles of connected trails across South Knoxville's ridge system. The Gateway trailhead is the main entry point and has parking, maps, and easy access to the full network. Great for a longer Saturday morning hike.
🗺️ Directions
Ijams Nature Center
🚗 5 min🐕 Dog Friendly⭐ 4.7
Knoxville's crown jewel nature park — 300 acres with trails, a visitor center, kayak/canoe rentals on the river, and stunning quarry swimming holes. Something for every energy level. One of the best places in the city.
🗺️ Directions
Fort Dickerson / Mead's Quarry
🚗 5 min⭐ 4.5 · Swimming
Stunning old quarry now open for swimming in brilliant blue-green water. Extremely popular with locals on hot days. The ruins and cliffs make it feel like a secret spot even though everyone knows about it. Bring towels.
🗺️ Directions
Suttree Landing Park
🚗 8 min🐕 Dog Friendly⭐ 4.6
Beautiful riverside park along the Tennessee River with greenway trails, a boat launch, and a great view of downtown across the water. Easy, flat walking — excellent option for the whole group including Grandpa.
🗺️ Directions
⛰️
Gatlinburg & the Smokies
Saturday big adventure · ~45 min drive
▼
Anakeesta
👧 Great for Kids⭐ 4.7 · Mountain theme park
Mountaintop adventure park accessible by gondola with treetop walks, zip lines, a chondola ride, and stunning 360° Smoky Mountain views. One of the most memorable experiences in the area — the kids will love it and the views are incredible for everyone.
🗺️ Directions
SkyLift Park & SkyBridge
👧 Kids Love It⭐ 4.5
America's highest pedestrian suspension bridge — 680 feet above Gatlinburg with jaw-dropping valley views. Reached via a classic chairlift. Takes about an hour total and is genuinely thrilling.
🗺️ Directions
Great Smoky Mountains National Park
⭐ Free to enter · Stunning scenic drives
The park entrance is minutes from Gatlinburg. For an accessible adventure, the Cades Cove Loop Road is a 11-mile one-way scenic drive with wildlife (bears, deer, elk), historic farmsteads, and pull-offs anyone can enjoy from the car or with minimal walking.
🗺️ Cades Cove
Ripley's Aquarium of the Smokies
👧 Kids' Favorite⭐ 4.6 · Gatlinburg
One of the top-rated aquariums in the country. Massive shark tunnel where you ride a moving walkway through the tank. Completely accessible, air-conditioned, and endlessly entertaining for all ages. A solid rainy-day backup too.
